# Anti-school

This repository exists to support **short, research-backed video narrations** that treat **compulsory schooling** as a serious moral and social problem—not a neutral background institution. The point is not “balance” for its own sake; it is to **document harms and structures** (labor, surveillance, testing markets, health, inequality) with **citations**, while holding a **clear ethical line** against mass compulsory schooling.

## Why this project

Mainstream discussion often assumes school is basically good, or at least inevitable. This project starts elsewhere: **children’s time and bodies** are conscripted for years of **mandatory attendance and compliance**, much of it **unpaid work**, under **carceral-style control** (movement, punishment, monitoring). We treat that arrangement as worth naming bluntly—**forced child labor**, **prison-like constraint**, **life-time wasted** in a system that **does not** earn the benefit of the doubt on **economic payoff**, **real learning**, or **human flourishing**.

We also treat schooling as **often hostile to depth**: **teach-to-test**, **forget**, **sleep loss**, **stress**, and—where research supports it—**mental health harm**. The repo is a workspace to turn those commitments into **scripts and evidence trails** that can survive scrutiny on camera.

## Values (summary)

The full, binding version for writers and editors is **`INTENT_AND_VALUES.md`**. In short:

- **Moral stance:** Compulsory school **wastes life**, extracts **unpaid labor**, and operates in ways we describe as **carceral**; it is **not** assumed to deliver **real economic or success value**, to be a reliable path to **intellectual progress**, or to produce **lasting learning** as usually practiced.
- **Epistemic stance:** Claims meant as **facts** need **scholarly or strong documentary support**. **Value judgments** belong in the project’s voice but must **not** masquerade as evidence. **Do not** soften the critique with **both-sides** comfort language that **contradicts** the positions above.
- **Accuracy:** Cite honestly; **do not overclaim** what studies show.

If you disagree with that stance, this is probably not the repo for you; if you agree and want to help **tighten arguments and sources**, you are welcome.

## What’s in the tree

- **`narrations/`** — Episode scripts (markdown); **one narration at a time** in workflow  
- **`research/`** — Research notes that feed episodes  
- **`books/`** — Quote extraction tooling and quote banks  

## Contribute

Pull requests and issues are welcome: **better citations**, **fact-checks**, **drafts or outlines**, **research briefs**, **small tooling fixes**. Read **`INTENT_AND_VALUES.md`** before you edit narrations. Prefer **one episode per PR** when possible, and say **what changed** and **what sources** back new factual claims.
