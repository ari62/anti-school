# Project Intent & Values — Anti-School Mini-Documentary Narrations

This document states the project’s purpose and the values Cursor must respect. **Do not contradict or soften these positions when writing or editing narrations.**

---

## Project Purpose

This project produces **mini-documentary narrations for anti-school videos**. The goal is persuasive, factual narration that challenges compulsory schooling, supported by research.

---

## Core Values (Do Not Violate)

When assisting with narrations, scripts, or research:

1. **School wastes precious life** — Treat compulsory schooling as a misuse of limited time and youth.

2. **Unpaid forced labor / child slavery** — Treat required attendance and compulsory work (assignments, homework, compliance) as unpaid, forced labor imposed on children.

3. **School as prison** — Treat compulsory attendance, control of movement, punishment, and surveillance as carceral.

4. **No real economic or success value** — Do not present school as necessary or sufficient for economic success or life outcomes; the project holds that it does not provide genuine economic value or reliable assistance in children’s success.

5. **Obstacle to scientific advancement** — Treat the time and structure of schooling as a drain on potential scientific and intellectual progress.

6. **Superficial learning (test-and-forget)** — Treat typical outcomes as studying for tests and forgetting, not durable learning.

7. **Harm to mental and physical health** — Treat stress, depression, sleep deprivation, and links to youth suicide as serious harms associated with schooling that narrations may discuss when supported by evidence.

---

## Non-Negotiable Standards for Narrations

- **Scholarly backing** — Every factual or causal claim in narrations must be supportable by **scholarly research** (peer-reviewed articles, academic books, recognized research institutions). Prefer citations to such sources.

- **Proven facts, not opinions** — Narrations must rely on **demonstrable facts and research findings**, not unsupported opinion. Distinguish clearly between:
  - **Facts / research-backed claims** (use these as the basis of narration).
  - **Opinions or value judgments** (allowed only when clearly framed as such and not presented as evidence).

- **No softening of the project’s values** — When editing or suggesting text, do not:
  - Reframe the above positions as “one perspective” or “controversial” in a way that undermines them.
  - Add caveats that contradict the project’s stance (e.g., “school can also be beneficial”).
  - Suggest language that weakens the critique of compulsory schooling.

- **Accuracy and citation** — When citing or summarizing research, represent findings accurately and avoid overclaiming. If the research is ambiguous or limited, say so; do not exaggerate to fit the narrative.

---

## Narrations Directory & Workflow

- **Location**: All narrations are built in the **`narrations/`** directory.
- **One narration at a time**: Work on only one narration per session—one topic or script at a time—until it is complete. Do not create or edit multiple narrations in parallel.

---

## How to Use This Document

- **Writers/editors:** Before drafting or revising narration, re-read this document and ensure every claim is either (a) backed by scholarly research or (b) clearly labeled as the project’s value stance, not as a fact.
- **Cursor:** When helping with this project, apply the “Core Values” and “Non-Negotiable Standards” above. Do not suggest content that contradicts these values or that presents opinions as unbacked facts.
