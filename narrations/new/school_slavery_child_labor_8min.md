# School as Slavery and Unpaid Child Labor

**~8 minutes | Mini-documentary narration**

**Angle:** Moral and research-backed frame: society dismisses compulsory schooling as “just school”; this episode treats it as required labor under penalty, the seizure of childhood time, and (in this project’s vocabulary) **unpaid child labor** and **child slavery**. The focus is **what is done to the child**; who captures the output is for a companion piece.

**Production note:** Read at a deliberate documentary pace (8.8–9.2 min with pauses; trim one quote block for a hard eight).

---

## INTRO - IF THIS WERE DONE TO ADULTS (0:00-0:55)

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**  
Imagine a law that forced adults to report to an institution most weekdays, stay there for hours, perform assigned mental labor on command, continue the work at home, and face penalties if they refused. 

No wage.  
No real negotiation.  
No meaningful exit.

If that happened to adults, people would not call it "opportunity."

They would call it coercion and slavery.

But when children are run through the same kind of custody, commands, and penalties, society changes the label and calls it **school and education**.

**Apologists** say compulsory school is **for the child's own good** and **for the economy**. Picture **mandatory daily gym** exercise for every adult, **sold** as **good for personal health** and **good for the economy** through **lower healthcare costs**. **No legislature would pass it.** **Children** already live under the **same logic**. **Mandatory gym** time would **provide benefits to the individual and society, however we do not mandate it because we value individual liberty. Compulsory schooling** does not help anyone. It causes **documented harm**, **years of wasted life**, and **no personal or economic return**.

That is the argument of this video: people call compulsory schooling **a helpful public service**. In plain moral terms, it is a system of **unpaid forced child labor** and **child slavery**. The hours are real. The work is real. The penalties are real.

The polite word is school. The reality is servitude.

## SECTION 1 - THE LABOR IS REAL, AND SO IS THE COMPULSION (0:55-3:00)

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**  
The first thing people do when you say school is child labor is pretend that schoolwork is not really work.

However, he child is working, just as an adult in a desk job is working. The labor is mental rather than manual: reading, writing, memorizing, solving, testing, studying, and producing homework, worksheets, and exams. School imposes that work under supervision, often with tighter controls and longer hours than many office jobs: bells, assigned seats, and forced silence. The child is not exempt from labor because the work happens sitting down, and the work does not have to be in a mine or factory for it to be child labor.

George Dennison, in *The Lives of Children*, described **who the room is built for** and **what happens to children's bodies and voices**: "School is established not in order that it should be convenient for the children to study, but that the teachers should be able to teach in comfort. The children's conversation, motion, and merriment, which are their necessary conditions of study, are not convenient for the teacher, and so in the schools—which are built on the plan of prisons—questions, conversation and motion are prohibited."

Children read, write, memorize, test, submit, and repeat. They are graded, ranked, punished, and compared. They do homework, study for and take exams, and comply with strict, dehumanizing, behavioral standards.

That is labor.

The coercion is real: **compulsory attendance**, **truancy laws**, **detentions**, **grade penalties**, **exclusion**, **humiliation**, **intimidation**, and **pressure on families**.

The **International Labour Organization** defines forced labor as work or service "exacted from any person under the menace of any penalty and for which the said person has not offered himself voluntarily."

That sentence stacks three conditions: **required work**, **penalty for refusal**, **no voluntary agreement**. School satisfies all three conditions.

John Holt attacked the moral core of that arrangement directly: "The idea of painless, non-threatening coercion is an illusion. Fear is the inseparable companion of coercion, and its inescapable consequence."

Ivan Illich speaks of one strategy coersion is achieved in *Deschooling Society*: "Only by segregating human beings in the category of childhood could we ever get them to submit to the authority of a schoolteacher."

**Labor** is commanded and controlled time and output under **someone else's** authority and schedule. When that control becomes forced and backed by punishment, it is **slavery**.

## SECTION 2 - THE HARM AND THE HOURS (3:00-4:20)

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**  
School causes proven harm. **Peer-reviewed studies** tie **school** to **depression, anxiety, and distress**. Examples include **Thomas Steare's** systematic review, *The Association Between Academic Pressure and Adolescent Mental Health Problems*, and **Viviana Wuthrich's** longitudinal study, *Tracking Stress, Depression, and Anxiety Across the Final Year of Secondary School*. **OECD PISA** analyses also connect **heavy study hours** to **weaker student well-being** in many countries.

And the hours are enormous. **OECD data** show that compulsory schooling alone consumes **thousands of hours** across primary and lower secondary years. Then evenings add homework, study, and test prep. **Those unpaid hours** of a child's life are simply **consumed**, and no long-term financial benefit is gained, as shown for example by **Pischke and von Wachter's** study, *Zero Returns to Compulsory Schooling in Germany: Evidence and Interpretation*.

Despite the claim that children are being educated, research shows what is learned for a class or test is forgotten soon after it passes. Dunlosky et al. on effective learning techniques, Karpicke and Roediger on retrieval practice, and Roediger and Butler on long-term retention all point this out.

Children’s health is harmed by sleep deprivation and prolonged forced sitting, both of which carry documented physical and cognitive costs with little demonstrated benefit, as shown for example by *Insufficient Sleep in Adolescents: Causes and Consequences* (Owens and Weiss) and *Physical Activity During School Recess: A Systematic Review* (Ridgers, Salmon, Parrish, Stanley, and Okely).

School is not just a building; it is a claim on years of a child's life.

When **forced labor** is fused with **custody**, **punishment**, and the **near-total seizure of time**, **it is slavery.** 

---

## SECTION 3 - COMPULSORY SCHOOLING IS CHILD SLAVERY (4:20-6:10)

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
**Slavery** and **child slavery** are deliberate moral terms here.  Forced output, punishment for refusal, no wage, no contract the child chose. 

John Taylor Gatto: "School is like starting life with a 12-year jail sentence in which bad habits are the only curriculum truly learned."

John Holt: "No one is more truly helpless, more completely a victim, than he who can neither choose nor change nor escape his protectors."

Children are compelled. They work. They are not paid. Their time is not theirs.

The school system wants you to call that "education." **Instead, it is** years of captive labor under threat. **That** is slavery.

---

## OUTRO - THE REAL NATURE OF SCHOOL (6:10-8:05)

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**  
Compulsory schooling confines and compels. It takes years of life, harms children mentally and physically, extends work into the home, punishes refusal, and treats children as the owned property of the state and the parents tricked into believing school is helpful.

That is **unpaid child labor and slavery**. It is **forced labor** and **captive time**, and there is **no real way out**. **Compulsory attendance** and truancy enforcement leave children and families with **compliance** as the only legally safe option.

"Education — compulsory schooling, compulsory learning — is a tyranny and a crime against the human mind and spirit. Let all those escape it who can, any way they can." (John Holt, *Instead of Education*)

**END**
