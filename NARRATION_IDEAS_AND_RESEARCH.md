### 2. School causes adolescent sleep deprivation (early starts, homework, studying late, and related pressures)
**Angle:** Treat sleep loss as a **systemic** outcome of schooling: circadian clash from **early start times**, **evening homework and cramming** that push bedtimes later, **test/exam periods**, heavy **course loads** and extracurriculars, and (where relevant) **screen-based schoolwork**—not a personal failure to “manage time.”  
**Research base:** Adolescent sleep biology and chronotype; policy studies on start times; cohort and cross-sectional work linking **academic workload/homework time** to shorter sleep and mediating mental-health pathways; systematic reviews on sleep deprivation in youth.

### 59. Compulsory schooling as unpaid child labor and child slavery
**Angle (project framing):** The state and guardians compel children into all-day custody for most of the year, for many years. Inside that custody they are ordered to perform cognitive and physical labor—seatwork, drills, homework, compliance rituals, high-stakes tests—with **no wage**, no contract they chose, and **sanctions** (grades, exclusion, detention, suspension, truancy enforcement against child and parent) if they refuse or fail. Total **compelled hours** include **in-school instruction time** plus **homework and study** that the system assigns. Narration treats this structure as **unpaid forced child labor** and **child slavery** in the project’s moral vocabulary (see **INTENT_AND_VALUES.md**).
